Showing Your Home

It’s not as easy as it may seem. Potential buyers have a tendency to look in every nook and cranny. They will look in your closets, your cabinets, and under your beds. Follow these simple guidelines to make showing your home a success!

  1. Make a Good First Impression. Have your lawn freshly cut and the landscaping neatly manicured.
  2. Eliminate Distractions. Keep the entryway clear of any umbrellas or shoes. Try adding flowers for a light, airy look.
  3. Less is More. Make your house the neatest it’s ever been. Clear countertops of clutter, including small appliances. Re-shelve books and display only minimal knick-knacks, so the potential buyers can visualize their own belongings in these spaces.
  4. Make Space. Showing plenty of floor and wall space will make your home feel bigger.
  5. Spic and span. Nobody likes a dirty house. Clean your house from top to bottom. It may be worthwhile to hire a cleaning crew, so you can focus your attention on other parts of the moving process.
  6. Be a Show Off. Highlight every feature of your house without shame – porches, alcoves, garages, and other storage areas are good selling features, but make sure these spaces are neat and clean.
  7. Closet Case. Make closets look organized and spacious. Keep as many belongings off the floor as possible, and make sure they are neatly vacuumed.
  8. Lighten Up. Light gives the appearance of space. Open curtains and clean skylights. Remove anything that blocks the windows, and make sure all light fixtures have new, brightly burning bulbs.
